Program Overview

The Differentiated HIV Services Program

A community-led, technology-enabled HIV response program across Malaysia's Key Populations — rooted in dignity, confidentiality, and the 95-95-95 target.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Person-Centred Care

Every client's privacy and confidentiality is protected.

🌱

Community-Led Expertise

CHWs and CHNs are picked among community members.

🔒

Confidential & Stigma-Free

Safe access to testing and care, no judgement.

🎯

95-95-95 Target

Driving Malaysia towards achieving NSPEA and UNAIDS global targets.

Component 1

DHSKP & Community-Based Testing (CBT)

Led by Community Health Workers (CHWs) serving Malaysia's affected Key Populations.

MSM
TG
WAR
PWUD
  • HIV screening via finger-prick and oral rapid tests
  • CHWs are certified by MOH via JKN to become the CBT Officers
  • Link-to-Care for reactive clients
  • Daily data entry via MyVAS & HIVnext
🏅 CBT Certified by MOH via JKN
Component 2

TestNow

A digital HIV self-testing platform enabling private, discreet access to testing kits.

  • Order oral and blood-based self-test kits online
  • Built-in HIV risk assessment tool
  • Empowers individuals to test privately
  • Promoted through various channels including MySejahtera, social media and digital platforms
Component 3

Community-Based Care (CBC)

Led by Community Health Navigators (CHNs) supporting clients through government clinic pathways.

  • PrEPGov access and follow-up
  • HIV and STI treatment linkage & support
  • Client navigation at Klinik Kesihatan
  • Guidance through Test → Treat → Suppress
Component 4

KAMI — Kesihatan Anak Muda IPT

Youth-focused program using Peer Educators to reach university students and young KP members.

  • Peer Educator-led outreach on campuses
  • Promotes TestNow self-testing platform
  • HIV/STI awareness and peer-to-peer guidance
  • Targets youth and young KP populations
Component 5

Digital Outreach

Online outreach via social media and digital platforms targeting Key Populations where they are.

  • Social media and dating apps outreach
  • Targets MSM, TG, WAR, and PWUD online
  • Links to DHSKP, TestNow, and CBC services
  • Reduces stigma through online engagement

Step-by-Step Workflow

Updating Link to Care Data

Each tab in the database serves a specific purpose. Follow the guide for each tab below — click a tab to see its instructions, images, and downloadable guide.

ℹ️ What is actionTask? This is your master pending list — all reactive HIV, SYP, and HEP C cases that are still awaiting Link to Care confirmation.
1
Open the actionTask tab

Navigate to the actionTask tab in your NGO's database. You will see a list of all pending reactive cases across all three disease categories.

2
Review pending cases

Each row represents a client with a reactive result who has not yet been confirmed as linked to care. Review the client's IC number, test date, and disease category.

3
Use the Notes column

If a case cannot yet be resolved — for example the client is unreachable — enter a justification in the Notes column. This keeps the audit trail clean and transparent.

4
Act on each case

For each case you are ready to update, proceed to the relevant tab — HIV, SYP, or HEP C — to complete the Link to Care entry.

ℹ️ HIV Tab — Update Link to Care status for all reactive HIV cases. Data entered here will be automatically pushed to dailyData at the next system shift.
1
Navigate to the HIV tab

Click the HIV tab in your NGO database. You will see all active HIV reactive clients pending Link to Care confirmation.

2
Select the client's IC number

In the IC Number column, use the drop-down menu to select the correct client. Do not type the IC manually — always use the dropdown to ensure data integrity.

3
Update editable columns

Fill in the remaining editable columns: Link to Care status, date linked, facility name, and any relevant remarks. Non-editable columns are locked and auto-populated by the system.

4
Wait for the next system shift

Your entry will be automatically extracted and pushed to the dailyData snapshot at the next Morning or Afternoon shift. No manual refresh needed.

⚠️ Important: The 2024 and 2025 tabs contain historical data for clients who still need to be linked to care. Update statuses directly in the columns marked in Coral Pink — these are the only editable fields in the historical tabs.
1
Navigate to the 2024 or 2025 tab

These tabs hold historical records pre-loaded from previous programme cycles. Each row represents a client who was reactive but whose Link to Care status was not completed at the time.

2
Identify the Coral Pink columns

Look for columns highlighted in Coral Pink (#ff7f50) — these are the only fields you should edit. All other columns are locked historical records and must not be modified.

3
Update the Link to Care status directly

Unlike the current-year tabs, historical data does not use a dropdown. Type or select the status directly in the Coral Pink column for each client row you are updating.

4
Historical data syncs at next shift

Updated historical records are included in the next ETL pipeline run. The dashboard's year-on-year comparison view will reflect your changes at the next Morning or Afternoon shift.

✅ Historical data from 2024 and 2025 is included in the dashboard's comparison charts, allowing you to evaluate current performance against previous years in real time.
